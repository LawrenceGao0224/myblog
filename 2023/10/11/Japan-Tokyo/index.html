Preparation

flight bookings: Remember to book the tickets correctly, especially the name spelling and passport number, because I typed my girlfriend’s name wrongly until the day before the flight we realized.

hotels: Because the great hotels are quickly to be fully booked. We used booking.com and agoda.com to compare the price and make a reservation.

Weather: When we arrived, it was really hot but 3 days after, it went to be chill at about 20 degree Celsius.

Day schedule

Day 1 : Taiwan to Japan

Our plane is departed at 14:25, and arrival time is 18:50.

While we arrived at Japan (成田国際空港) Narita International Airport, my suitcase was broken. ****We checked in the hotel first and went to a barbeque restaurant nearby called 一頭牛燒肉 房家, it was amazing.

一頭牛燒肉 房家

Day 2 : 鎌倉 Kamakura & 江ノ島 Enoshima

It’s a cool coastal town just an hour from Tokyo by train. We kicked off at 8:00 AM, and the streets were still pretty quiet.

We made a stop at this cute bakery inside 品川 Shinagawa station for some yummy mini sandwiches. Can you believe how fresh and tasty they were? Oh, and remember, no munching while walking in Japan, so we sat down to enjoy.

By 10 AM, we hit Kamakura, the weather was kinda cloudy, but it felt nice. As soon as you step out the station, you’ll spot this Torii gate marking the start of the famous 小町Komachi Dori shopping street.

小町通

We tried matcha ice cream for the first time in Japan, and it was super tasty on a chilling day like this!

The shops here were adorable, selling local goodies like scents, crystal jewelry, honey, and more.

And don't miss out on this Good Luck Buddha cake. It's fluffy with a sweet filling. Each flavor brings luck in a different area of life – and they're delicious!
Further down, we came across the **鶴岡八幡宮**Shirahata shrine. It had this ancient, serene vibe that made us feel so calm.

鶴岡八幡宮

Next up was the Great Buddha at 鎌倉大佛殿高德院Kotokuin temple. This spot is a must-see, full of vibes and history that are totally special.

鎌倉大佛殿高德院

And then, I tried these sweet soy sauce dumplings. The owner was super kind and even treated us to some free matcha tea.

If you’re into anime, you might recognize the famous 鐮倉高校前Kamakurakokomae crossing. We got there at noon, so getting a pic without someone’s head in it was a bit of a challenge. Tip: for awesome pics, go early on a weekday!

[鐮倉高校前](https://maps.app.goo.gl/XqBm51KXiKMDJLnx9)

I was starving, so we hit a local ramen shop. My girlfriend went for beef rice, and I got soy sauce ramen - both hit the spot.

Enoshima Black Ramen MENGA

Finally, we wrapped up the day at Enoshima Island, right around sunset. The view was absolutely breathtaking! The island’s connected by a bridge and has so much to explore. There are tons of souvenir shops by those torii gates and on the road leading to Enoshima Shrine. And trust me, the treats here are unique and totally worth a try!

[江之島大橋](https://maps.app.goo.gl/HcXBxzkaZze8rb767)

Heading back to Ueno, we had grilled eel over rice for dinner, it’s really delicious. Let’s call it a day!

Day 3 : 銀座 Ginza & Teamlab

We visited the 皇居 Kokyo Higashi Gyoen Garden -Imperial Palace East Garden nearby Tokyo station. It is spectacular but quiet, however, we did not get inside, just walked around the garden.

[皇居](https://maps.app.goo.gl/PN9RiVsrxWGHEPGX7)

By 10 am, we arrived at Ginza, walking all around the streets. There’s a street at specific moment can only be passengers walking without any vehicles. It’s full of luxury stores and famous restaurants. We entered the greatest Uniqlo and MUJI, they got everything there. I bought some clothes there, it’s kind of the paradise of shopping.

We had a really local Ramen nearby, the soup is really tense, I love it but my girlfriend felt salty.
[銀座 嚆矢](https://maps.app.goo.gl/VuGF1eRXcUuzV2vY9)

Finally, we went to Teamlab(remember to book it beforehand, or there would be a long wait). There were full of people, but it’s really worthy. You need to immerse yourself into the whole area, and it’s kind of mysterious and interesting. You can feel the lights and the sounds with your body, and the touching of the ground, the temperature of the water and the floor. It’s my first time having this kind of experience, everything is miracle for me.

[teamLab Planets](https://maps.app.goo.gl/LL7ML45QgBjLUuDj9)

Day 4 : 原宿、新宿 + 涉谷

We arrived at 明治神宮 Shinto Shrine at around 9 am. The whole area is quiet but it’s full of people, and the temple is peaceful and it is known for the 明治維新, the goal was to combine “modern advances” with traditional “eastern” values.

[明治神宮](https://maps.app.goo.gl/m3KBL9d2bpvWaz3j9)

At 10:30 am, we went shopping in some second-hand clothes and shops, but I did not buy any clothes there. But I recommended everyone to come and visit, there are many beautiful streets and roads hidden in some places.

We had sushi for lunch, it is not easy to find and the place is small, but it’s high CP. We didn’t wait too long but when we finished, there are many people waiting outside.
[Sanaka](https://maps.app.goo.gl/M7ZXdKsVyo7PAztS7)

We then visited Shibuya at around 3:30pm. There are tons of people there, that every seconds every moment, people are passing through the Shibuya cross. We went to some shops and Disney store. Finally, I bought a shoes in ABC-Mart, it’s really cheap and worth buying. We were planning to go onto the Shibuya sky, but it’s full, so we decide to sit in the Starbucks right above the Shibuya cross, seeing everyone passing through is another interesting activity.

[渋谷スクランブル交差点](https://maps.app.goo.gl/NnqAayUgA52uWddB6)

We headed back to Ueno, and had fried pork chops. It’s really delicious and cheap. Let’s call it a day.

[とんかつ山家 上野店](https://maps.app.goo.gl/h6yDvymHHUDTseWN6)
